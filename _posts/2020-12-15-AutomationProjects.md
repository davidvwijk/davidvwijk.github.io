I don't like doing menial tasks, so I often find myself automating things. It usually turns out that it would have been quicker to just do the menial task, but I digress. Below are some quick automation projects that I used to pass the time during lockdown. 

## COVID-19 Vaccination Appointment Bot

In the spring of 2021 when vaccines were first starting to become available, I was eager to help my parents find an available appointment once they became eligible. I created a short script to crawl the New York State Vaccine website for our county and signed up for a free Twilio account to send me texts and also send my parents emails upon the discovery of an open vaccine slot.

## DailyChecker Bot

During my senior year at Cornell, Covid was in full effect. We were required to fill out a "daily check" form every single day, regardless of whether we had in-person classes or not. The questions were the same every single day and although it only took a few minutes to complete, it was tiresome, especially since I seldom went on campus those semesters. So I decided to make a script that would sign in and complete the form for me every single day, at a random time during the morning, and then text me once it was finished for the day. Again, I used selenium and the free Twilio trial account I had set up to send the text messages.
